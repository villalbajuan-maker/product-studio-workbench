# Bolt Prompt

Use this prompt to generate the first landing page.

```text
Build a polished but lean landing page for a new B2B productized service / product studio validation offer.

The goal of this landing page is not to explain a company or brand. The goal is to validate a commercial offer and get qualified B2B service companies to book a conversation around one real ambiguous opportunity or project.

Core positioning:
"Turn ambiguous opportunities into defensible scopes before delivery starts."

Audience:
Growing B2B service companies with consultative sales and complex delivery. These are agencies, studios, consultancies, implementation firms, professional service companies, and boutique service businesses where founders, partners, or senior leaders still have to interpret opportunities, rescue scopes, or translate between sales and delivery.

Problem:
Many B2B service companies do not lose margin because delivery is weak. They lose margin because opportunities enter delivery before the problem, scope, assumptions, risks, and success criteria are clear. By the time delivery starts, ambiguity has already become rework, margin loss, senior dependency, and delivery risk.

Offer:
A Pre-Delivery Decision Map. We take one real opportunity or project before the team is committed and turn it into a clearer problem, economic context, delivery risks, defensible scope, proportional next step, and handoff for sales and delivery.

Primary conversion:
Book a call / review a real opportunity.

Tone:
Clear, sharp, senior, operational, commercially grounded. Avoid hype, generic SaaS language, startup cliches, "AI transformation," "unlock your potential," "scale your business," or vague consulting language.

Visual direction:
Professional, modern, focused, and calm. Not flashy. Not corporate generic. Avoid purple/blue gradients, dark navy SaaS themes, beige/tan palettes, and decorative gradient blobs. Use a clean light background with strong typography, black/charcoal text, one crisp accent color such as green, red, or electric cyan, and subtle borders. Border radius should be modest, 8px or less. Use plenty of whitespace. The page should feel like a serious decision tool, not a marketing template.

Layout:
Create a single-page landing page with these sections:

1. Hero
Headline:
"Turn ambiguous opportunities into defensible scopes before delivery starts."

Subheadline:
"We help growing B2B service companies reduce rework, senior dependency, and margin loss when unclear opportunities move from sales into delivery."

Primary CTA:
"Review a real opportunity"

Secondary CTA:
"View sample map"

Add a small credibility line under the CTAs:
"For agencies, consultancies, studios, implementation firms, and B2B service teams with complex delivery."

2. Problem Section
Title:
"The problem does not start in delivery."

Body:
"It starts earlier, when an opportunity enters the pipeline with unclear assumptions, vague scope, solution-shaped requests, or expectations nobody has fully unpacked. By the time delivery gets involved, the team is already paying for the ambiguity."

Add three concise pain cards:
- "Senior leaders keep translating the real problem."
  "Founders, partners, or leads become the bridge between sales, clients, and delivery."
- "Delivery inherits vague promises."
  "The team starts with missing context, weak scope, and assumptions that should have been resolved earlier."
- "Margin leaks through rework."
  "Ambiguity turns into extra meetings, scope drift, repeated clarification, and delivery rescue work."

3. What We Do
Title:
"Before delivery starts, we turn ambiguity into a decision."

Body:
"We take one real opportunity or project and create a Pre-Delivery Decision Map: a structured view of the problem, value at stake, risks, scope, next step, and handoff."

Show a horizontal or vertical process:
Initial request -> Reframed problem -> Value at stake -> Delivery risks -> Defensible scope -> Proportional next step -> Sales/delivery handoff

4. Sample Artifact Preview
Title:
"See what the decision map looks like."

Create a large embedded preview panel that looks like an actual product artifact, not a PDF. It should look like a private URL/report page.

Use this sample case:
"A consulting firm receives a client request to 'automate client onboarding.' Sales wants to move fast. Delivery is worried the scope is vague. The founder would normally step in to translate what the client actually needs."

Include sections for Executive Recommendation, Initial Request, Reframed Problem, Observed Signals, Likely Cause, Value At Stake, Risks If Misframed, Defensible Scope, Proportional Next Step, Success Criteria, Sales/Delivery Handoff, and Guided Questions.

Guided Questions:
- "Why should we not build yet?"
- "What must sales clarify before closing?"
- "What does delivery need to protect margin?"
- "Which assumptions could break the scope?"
- "What is the proportional next step?"

Important: This should not be a functional AI chatbot yet. It should visually suggest that the artifact can be explored, but do not overbuild the feature.

5. Who It Is For
Title:
"For service companies where opportunity quality determines delivery margin."

Bullets:
- "You sell custom or semi-custom B2B services."
- "Your sales process is consultative."
- "Delivery depends on context, judgment, and clear scope."
- "Senior people still rescue too many opportunities."
- "You suspect margin is being lost before delivery even begins."

6. Outcomes
Title:
"What changes after the map?"

Use outcome cards:
- "Clearer scope"
- "Less senior dependency"
- "Better delivery handoff"
- "Smarter next step"

7. CTA Section
Headline:
"Bring one ambiguous opportunity."

Body:
"We will help you turn it into a clearer delivery decision before your team pays for the ambiguity."

CTA:
"Review a real opportunity"

Footer:
"Built for B2B service companies that want better delivery decisions before delivery starts."

Functionality:
- Single-page responsive design.
- CTA buttons can link to a placeholder such as "mailto:" or "#contact".
- Add a simple contact form at the bottom with fields:
  Name, company, email, type of service business, "What opportunity or project feels ambiguous right now?"
- Add smooth scroll from CTAs to the form or sample map.
- Make the sample artifact section feel like the hero proof of the page.
- Ensure mobile layout is excellent.
- Do not include pricing.
- Do not mention AI, automation, software development, product studio, agency, or consulting as the main offer.
- Do not create a generic SaaS dashboard.
- Do not create a large navigation menu. Keep it simple: Problem, Map, Outcomes, CTA.

Deliverable:
A complete landing page ready to run locally, with clean component structure and polished responsive styling.
```
