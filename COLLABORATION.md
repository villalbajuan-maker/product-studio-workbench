# Collaboration

This repository is meant to be a shared construction space, not just a codebase.

It should help collaborators understand:

- what we believe;
- what we have learned;
- what is being validated now;
- where their judgment can improve the project.

## Current Roles

### Juan

Primary responsibilities:

- shape the product thesis;
- design the system logic;
- build the landing and artifacts;
- produce the workbench assets;
- turn ambiguous ideas into usable product surfaces;

Primary responsibilities:

- test the language against the market;

- bring cultural and buyer-context judgment;
- validate whether the offer resonates in the U.S. service market;
- sell the first pilots;
- help translate the thesis into buyer conversations.

## Shared Goal

The goal is not to create a beautiful repo, a clever landing page, or a theoretical studio.

The goal is to validate whether this thesis can become a business:

> Can we help B2B service companies make better pre-delivery decisions, and will they pay for it?

## How To Contribute

Contributions are most useful when they improve one of these:

1. **Commercial clarity**
   - Is the landing understandable?
   - Would a buyer recognize the pain?
   - Does the language sound natural in the U.S. market?

2. **Buyer access**
   - Who should we talk to?
   - Which founders, partners, or service companies fit the ICP?
   - What warm introductions are possible?

3. **Offer sharpness**
   - Is the Pre-Delivery Decision Map clear?
   - Is the output valuable enough to pay for?
   - What would make the pilot easier to buy?

4. **Artifact quality**
   - Does the sample map look like a useful decision tool?
   - Does it feel productized rather than like a generic report?
   - What would make it more credible?

5. **Validation discipline**
   - Are we talking to buyers fast enough?
   - Are we avoiding overbuilding?
   - Are we learning from real conversations?

## Working Rule

> The repo should preserve learning, but it should not become a place to hide from selling.

Documentation is useful only if it helps us make better decisions, sell better, or build better.
