# Problem Hypothesis

## Frozen Version

> Organizations in growth or change face recurring signals of disorder, inefficiency, or opportunity, but lack a reliable capacity to turn those signals into clearly formulated problems, defensible priorities, and actions proportional to the economic value at stake.

## Why This Is Clean

- It does not mention technology.
- It does not mention AI.
- It does not mention agencies or development.
- It does not assume the answer is software.
- It treats disorder, inefficiency, and opportunity as signals, not causes.
- It anchors the problem before resource commitment.

## Validation Criteria

A better future version must improve at least two of these without seriously degrading any:

- problem cleanliness;
- subject precision;
- observability;
- economic clarity;
- falsifiability;
- explanation of the failed conversion;
- preservation of proportionality.

## Master Question

> What structural failure prevents an organization from converting recurring signals of disorder, inefficiency, or opportunity into decisions and actions that are proportionally valuable?
