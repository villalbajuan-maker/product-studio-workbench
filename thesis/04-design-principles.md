# Design Principles

These principles define what any future product, service, studio process, or venture must obey.

## Principles V0.1

1. The signal is not the problem.
2. The requested solution should be treated as suspicious, not automatically obeyed.
3. Causality must precede intervention.
4. Economic value must be explicit before resource commitment.
5. Action must be proportional to the value at stake.
6. Formulation must increase effective speed, not slow it down.
7. Priorities must be defensible, not merely decided.
8. Success must be defined as change, not delivery.
9. Interpretation must be shared when the problem crosses functions.
10. Reasoning must be preserved.

## Mother Principle

> Before committing resources, the organization must understand what signal it is reading, what problem it represents, what economic value is at stake, and what size of action is appropriate.

## Antithesis

> We must not become a machine for turning ambiguous briefs into fast deliverables.
